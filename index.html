I'm Kabir, and this is a collection of my academic and personal work.

The majority of my work deals with how econometrics and statistics relate to the field of urban planning. I'm especially interested in applying spatial analysis methods to economic development and transportation issues.

Currently, I'm pursuing my Master's in Urban Planning and Policy at the University of Illinois Chicago. During this time, I'm working as a research assistant at the Urban Spatial Structure Lab and the Great Cities Institute at the College of Urban Planning and Policy Affairs.


Latest Project

The Lower West Side (Pilsen): History, Demographics, and Gentrification

During the Fall '24 semester at UIC, I prepared this memorandum on Pilsen as a part of the course UPP 501: Space, Place, and Institutions. This memorandum looks at the history, demographics, and physical structure of the Lower West Side and incorporates stakeholder interviews to identify reasons for and indicators of gentrification in the neighborhood.
